During the last 20 years,
461 persons lost their lives in an avalanche accident in Switzerland.

Learn what are the important factors in the formation of avalanches through the deadly avalanche data from the last 20 years in Switzerland coupled with meteorological data.

Avalanches are caused by an instability in the snowpack. This happens more often on north facing slopes which are never warmed by the sun.

However on sunny spring days the molten snow can also cause wet snow avalanches.
The wind carries the snow and forms a compact layer that does not solidarize well with the old snow.
Avalanches are often triggered in those fragile layers called wind plates.
Fresh snow needs time to solidarize with the old snowpack. The first days after a snowfall are especially dangerous.
The avalanche risk increases with altitude as there is more snow and wind.

Tours at altitudes higher than 3000 meters are less frequent which is why the number of avalanches decreases.
Finally, human decisions play a key role in many avalanche accidents.
Being part of a group can give a false impression of security.

One third of the mortal avalanches happened on a limited risk day.
